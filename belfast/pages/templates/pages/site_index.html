The Belfast Group connected a new generation of poets and writers from Northern Ireland.

Meeting at Philip Hobsbaum’s house in the shadow of Queen’s University, the Group read and commented on the drafts of each others’ poems, plays, and stories. The most notable members of the Group included Seamus Heaney, Paul Muldoon, and Michael Longley. Read drafts from these poets and others.

Interactive networks and digitized drafts of poetry make the Belfast Group's connections visible.

After the Group stopped meeting in 1972, many participants downplayed its importance to their writing. But by measuring when these authors wrote to each other—in poems or letters—we can better understand their connections.
